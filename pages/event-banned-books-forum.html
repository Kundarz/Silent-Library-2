July 15, 2025 | 6:00 PM – 9:00 PM

Join us at Silent Library for a thought-provoking evening as we host the Banned Book Forum, a powerful discussion and exhibition centered around the history, controversy, and cultural impact of banned and challenged books from around the world.

From George Orwell’s 1984 to Toni Morrison’s Beloved, the forum features banned books across decades, cultures, and genres. Why were they censored? Who decides what’s acceptable? How have these books shaped political, racial, and social dialogue?

The evening will include a panel featuring Dr. Lena Ko, an expert in literary censorship, alongside notable educators and student activists. Guests are invited to share their own experiences with banned or challenged books, and discuss how freedom of information relates to democracy and literacy.

Whether you’re a reader, a teacher, or simply curious, the Banned Book Forum is a rare opportunity to critically engage with the literature that pushed boundaries and questioned norms. Refreshments will be provided, and each attendee will receive a free bookmark listing the top 10 most challenged books of the decade.

Don’t miss this important conversation at the intersection of literature and liberty. Doors open at 5:30 PM. Seating is limited — arrive early to reserve your spot.

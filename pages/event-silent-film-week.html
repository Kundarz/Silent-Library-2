August 12–18, 2025 | Various Times

Experience the magic of the silver screen in a unique setting with Silent Library’s Silent Film Week, a curated festival celebrating the art and history of silent cinema. From August 12th through 18th, join fellow cinephiles and literature lovers for daily screenings of iconic silent films, accompanied by live music and immersive storytelling.

This event invites attendees to journey back to cinema’s earliest days, when stories were told without dialogue, relying on expressive acting, dramatic gestures, and evocative visuals. Films will be screened in the library’s intimate theater room, transformed with vintage décor and mood lighting inspired by the 1920s and 30s.

Each evening features a different theme — from slapstick comedy classics by Charlie Chaplin and Buster Keaton, to haunting dramas, and pioneering works by early female directors. Silent Library’s resident pianist will perform live scores, enhancing the mood and bridging the gap between film and audience.

In addition to film screenings, the week offers workshops and talks on silent film history, restoration efforts, and the intersection of early cinema and literature. Participants will learn how silent storytelling techniques influenced modern narrative forms across media.

Visitors can explore an exhibit showcasing vintage film posters, original scripts, and memorabilia from the silent era. Silent Library’s rare book collection will be available for browsing, featuring early screenplays and writings by notable silent film pioneers.

Complementing the experience, themed refreshments inspired by the Roaring Twenties will be served — including classic cocktails, mocktails, and finger foods. Attendees are encouraged to dress in period attire to fully embrace the nostalgic atmosphere.

Silent Film Week is more than just a film festival; it’s a celebration of storytelling’s evolution, a testament to how silence can speak volumes. Whether you’re a seasoned film buff or curious newcomer, this week promises unforgettable moments of connection, culture, and cinematic wonder.
